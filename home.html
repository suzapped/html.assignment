Air Pollution

According to WHO, outdoor air pollution is estimated to have caused 4.2 million premature deaths worldwide in 2019.
Air pollution is one of the greatest environmental risks to child health. Hence, the urgent call to shift to cleaner heavy-duty vehicles and low emission vehicles.

Visit WHO

Pollutants with the strongest evidence for public health concern

  • Particulate matter (PM)
  • Carbon monoxide (CO)
  • Nitrogen dioxide (NO2)
  • Ozone (O3)
Pollutants Sources Health effects
Particulate matter Traffic and transportation, industrial activities, power plants etc. PM is capable of penetrating deep into the lung and enter the bloodstream causing cardiovascular, cerebrovascular and respiratory impacts
Carbon monoxide The predominant source of carbon monoxide (CO) in ambient air is from motor vehicles. Exposure to carbon monoxide can cause difficulties breathing, exhaustion, dizziness, and other flu-like symptoms.
Nitrogen dioxide Ambient sources of NO2 results from high temperature combustion of fuels used for transportation, power generation etc. NO2 is an important ozone precursor, a pollutant closely linked to asthma and other respiratory conditions.
